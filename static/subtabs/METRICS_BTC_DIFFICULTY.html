Bitcoin Difficulty Across Multiple Time Frames

About Bitcoin Difficulty

Bitcoin difficulty measures how hard it is to find a valid block compared to the easiest possible difficulty defined by the protocol. It is a discrete network parameter that remains constant during a difficulty period and is recalculated every 2,016 blocks (≈ every 14 days) to keep the average block time close to 10 minutes.

Rising difficulty typically reflects sustained increases in total network hash power, while declining difficulty indicates reduced miner participation or hash rate withdrawal.
